Data Deletion

SpendSense — Android personal expense tracker
Published by AYCreation IT Services · Last updated: May 18, 2026

This page describes how to request deletion of your SpendSense data, what gets deleted, and what is retained.

What data we hold

SpendSense stores the following types of user data:

  • Local on-device data: transactions, budgets, goals, splits, achievements, app preferences, optional location tags, and any receipt images you have attached. Stored in an encrypted SQLCipher database and app-private file storage.
  • Cloud-synced data (only if you enabled cloud sync): amount, type, category, source, merchant, note, bank name, timestamp, edit-state, and currency for each transaction; category, limit, and month for each budget. Scoped to your Firebase user ID. Account numbers, raw SMS, receipts, and location are never synced.
  • Encrypted Drive backups (only if you enabled Drive backup): AES-256-encrypted backup blobs in your Google Drive's app-folder. These are stored in your Google Drive, not ours — only you can access them.
  • Trial-start anchor: an immutable Firestore document recording your Firebase user ID and the timestamp at which your free trial began. Retained permanently as an anti-fraud measure — see What is retained below.
  • Subscription billing records: held by Google Play, not by us. We do not store payment information.

How to delete your data

Option 1 — In-app account deletion (removes cloud data; signs you out)

  1. Open the SpendSense app.
  2. Go to More → Settings.
  3. Scroll to Account and tap Delete account.
  4. Confirm the deletion.

This action:

  • Deletes every transaction and budget document, plus your user record, from Firestore (immediately, or within 24 hours under poor network conditions).
  • Deletes your Firebase Auth account and signs you out of this device.
  • Revokes the Drive backup permission grant. Existing backup files remain in your Drive until you delete them manually — see Option 4 below.
  • Does not delete local data on this device. Your encrypted database and receipt images stay on the device so you can continue to use SpendSense in offline-only mode. To remove local data, also use Option 2 or Option 3.
  • Does not delete the trial-start anchor — see What is retained.

Option 2 — Clear app data (removes local data)

  1. Open Android Settings → Apps → SpendSense → Storage.
  2. Tap Clear data.

This deletes all on-device data (encrypted database, receipt images, preferences) immediately. Cloud-synced data and Drive backups are not affected — use Option 1 or Option 4 for those.

Option 3 — Uninstall (removes local data)

Uninstalling the app removes all local data, equivalent to Option 2. Cloud-synced data and Drive backups remain — use Option 1 before uninstalling, or contact us to remove them.

Option 4 — Delete Drive backups manually

  1. Open drive.google.com and sign in with the account you used for backup.
  2. Click the gear icon → Settings → Manage apps.
  3. Find SpendSense in the list.
  4. Click Options → Delete hidden app data.

Option 5 — Email request (fallback)

If you cannot use the in-app option (for example, you have already uninstalled), email us at info@aycreation.com with the subject line Data deletion request. We will respond within 7 business days and complete the deletion within 30 days.

To help us locate your records, please include in the email:
– the Google account email you used to sign in to SpendSense (if any)
– the approximate date you installed the app

What is retained, and for how long

  • Trial-start anchor: the document at users/{your-uid}/license/trial is retained permanently and cannot be deleted by users. It contains only your Firebase UID and a timestamp — no personal data — and exists solely to prevent trial reset via reinstall.
  • Subscription transaction records: retained by Google Play per their Play Billing terms — typically 7 years for tax/accounting compliance. We have no control over these. You can manage your subscriptions at play.google.com/store/account/subscriptions.
  • Anonymous server logs (if any): aggregated diagnostic logs from Firebase that do not identify you may be retained by Google for up to 90 days per their default settings.
  • Email correspondence: if you contact us via email, we retain the email thread for up to 12 months for support continuity, after which it is deleted.

Confirmation

For Option 1 (in-app), Firestore deletion is immediate for transactions and budgets, or completes within 24 hours under poor network conditions — no further confirmation is sent. For Option 5 (email), we will send you a confirmation email when deletion is complete.
