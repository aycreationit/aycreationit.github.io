Privacy Policy

SpendSense — Android personal expense tracker
Published by AYCreation IT Services · Last updated: May 18, 2026

This policy explains what data the SpendSense Android app collects, where it is stored, and your rights as a user. By installing the app you agree to the practices described below.

The short version

SpendSense is a local-first app. Your transactions, budgets, goals, splits, receipts, and any optional location tags live in an encrypted database on your device. SpendSense does not store your financial data on our backend — the only data the app sends to our backend is a single anti-fraud record described under What we send to our backend below: two timestamps and an anonymous, randomly-generated identifier with no link to your real identity. If you choose to enable the optional Drive Backup feature, encrypted backup blobs are written to your own Google Drive (see Encrypted Drive Backups). We do not maintain user accounts, do not collect personal information, and do not show ads.

Data we read from your device

SpendSense reads bank SMS messages and payment-app notifications on your device to automatically detect financial transactions. From those messages we extract: transaction amounts, merchant names, bank names, partial account numbers (typically the last 4 digits as they appear in the SMS), and timestamps.

We do not collect: full SMS bodies, OTP codes, passwords, names, email addresses, phone numbers, contacts, or any personally identifying information.

The app may also collect the following, only when you opt in to the respective feature:

  • Device location (latitude / longitude) captured at the moment a transaction is detected, used to tag the transaction on the map view. Location is captured once per transaction; the app does not track your location continuously in the background. You can revoke this permission in Android Settings at any time.
  • Receipt images you choose to attach to a transaction. Stored in app-private storage on this device. Not uploaded to our backend.

Where it is stored

All financial data is stored locally on your device in an encrypted database (SQLCipher, AES-256). The encryption key is derived from a per-install device identifier and never leaves your device. Your data does not leave your device except as described in the next two sections.

What we send to our backend

On first launch the app sends a single record to our backend: a random anonymous identifier (generated by Firebase, not linked to your real identity, Google account, email, phone, or device) and a timestamp marking when your free trial began. We retain this record permanently to prevent free-trial abuse via uninstall/reinstall — neither you nor we can delete it.

That's it. Your transactions, budgets, goals, splits, receipts, location data, and everything else stay on your device.

Encrypted Drive Backups (Optional, Premium)

SpendSense does not have its own user accounts — there is no sign-up or sign-in to SpendSense.

If you are a Premium subscriber and enable Drive Backup, the app writes AES-256-encrypted backup blobs to a hidden folder inside your own Google Drive. The encryption key stays on your device — we cannot read these backups, and we receive no information about your Google account or your Drive's other contents.

SMS & Notification Access

We request the READ_SMS and Notification Listener permissions solely to detect bank- and payment-app-originated transaction messages. We do not read personal SMS conversations, social-media notifications, or any non-financial messages. SMS bodies are processed on-device and discarded once the transaction is parsed; only the extracted fields (amount, merchant, bank, timestamp) are persisted, and only to your local encrypted database.

Advertising & Analytics

SpendSense does not display advertisements. We do not use Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any other analytics or tracking SDK. We do not share any data with advertising networks, analytics brokers, or third-party trackers.

Third-Party Services

SpendSense uses the following Google services:

Firebase Authentication — creates the anonymous user identifier described under What we send to our backend. See Google's privacy policy.
Google Firestore — stores only the anti-fraud record described above. See Firebase privacy.
Google Play Billing — for subscription purchases. We do not see or store your payment information. See Play terms.
Google Drive (optional, Premium only) — for encrypted backups, only if you enable Drive Backup.

Data Retention

On-device data is retained until you delete it via the app, clear app data from Android Settings, or uninstall the app. The anti-fraud record described above is retained permanently. We do not retain any other server-side records of your usage.

Data Deletion

You can remove your data by any of the following:

  1. Clear app data in Android Settings — removes all on-device data (encrypted database, receipt images, preferences, location tags). This is the fastest and most complete way to remove your data.
  2. Uninstall the app — equivalent to step 1.
  3. Delete account from in-app Settings — deletes the anonymous Firebase user created on first launch. The anti-fraud record described above is retained. This option does not remove local data; use step 1 or 2 to remove local data.
  4. Delete Drive backups (only if you have used Drive Backup) — go to drive.google.com → Settings → Manage apps → SpendSense → Delete hidden app data.

See the Data Deletion page for full instructions.

Children's Privacy

SpendSense is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with information, please contact us at the email below and we will delete it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via an in-app notice and the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Your Rights

Because SpendSense does not store your financial data on our servers, your data is fully under your control on this device:

  • Access & correction — every transaction is visible and editable in the app.
  • Export — SpendSense Premium subscribers can export to CSV or PDF directly from the in-app Settings. We do not retain a server-side copy of your data, so we cannot email you one — your device is the authoritative copy.
  • Deletion — see Data Deletion above. Because your data is stored only on this device, you can remove it instantly with Android's Clear App Data or by uninstalling the app — no server-side action required.
